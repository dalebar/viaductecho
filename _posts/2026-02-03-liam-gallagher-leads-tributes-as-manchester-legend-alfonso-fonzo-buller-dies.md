Alfonso "Fonzo" Buller, a "true Manchester legend," has sadly passed away at 65. He’s celebrated for crafting the iconic Manc swagger and popularising the phrase "Manchester Vibes in the Area," a motto embraced by artists like Liam Gallagher and Ian Brown. Fonzo was a key figure in the local music scene, launching the MVITA sound system in the 80s and 90s and shared the stage with stars such as Oasis and The Stone Roses. Known for his infectious energy and dance moves, Fonzo left a lasting impact on the music community, promoting love and unity through music.

His family, including his son Alonzo, spoke about their devastating loss and fondly remembered his vibrant spirit. Many tributes poured in, highlighting his role as a "living legend" and a force of nature in Manchester's cultural landscape. Friends and fellow artists reflected on his charisma and kindness, confirming plans for a celebration of his life in Manchester. Fonzo’s legacy will endure through his ten children and numerous grandchildren, ensuring the Manchester vibes he cherished continue to resonate. Remembered as a unique, loving character, his presence will be sorely missed.
