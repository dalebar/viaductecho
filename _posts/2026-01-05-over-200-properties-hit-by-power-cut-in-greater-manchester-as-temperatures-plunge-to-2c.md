On Sunday evening, over 250 properties in Tameside, Greater Manchester, lost power as temperatures dipped to -2C. The outage, affecting areas near Mottram Road in Hyde, was caused by an unexpected issue with an underground cable, according to Electricity North West. The cut was first reported around 5pm, with hopes to restore power by 11pm. Electricity North West reassured residents that they were working swiftly to fix the problem but noted that the repair time might vary if additional complications arose during the process. A comprehensive list of affected postcodes was provided, emphasising the widespread impact of the outage.
