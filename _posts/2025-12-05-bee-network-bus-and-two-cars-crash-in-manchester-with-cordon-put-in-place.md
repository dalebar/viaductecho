Emergency services rushed to a busy junction in Wythenshawe, Manchester, after a crash involving two cars and a Bee Network bus around 8am on December 5. The vehicles included a black Volkswagen Golf and a black Mercedes-Benz SUV. Firefighters worked on the Mercedes, using equipment to remove part of it. A cordon was established at the scene, but thankfully, no serious injuries were reported, and no arrests were made. Transport for Greater Manchester confirmed that bus passengers were unharmed, and North West Ambulance Service also responded. Fire crews from Wythenshawe attended the incident, ensuring the area was safe for about 45 minutes.
