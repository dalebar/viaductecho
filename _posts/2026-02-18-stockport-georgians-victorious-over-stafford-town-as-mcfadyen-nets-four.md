Stockport Georgians had a thrilling away match against Stafford Town, coming back to win 6-4 despite a rocky start. After a 1-1 draw in their last meeting, Georgians had some changes in the lineup, with young defender Tom O'Brien starting and goalkeeper Morgan Piper returning from injury. The match started poorly with Stafford scoring first, but Georgians quickly equalised with a penalty after Ollie McFadyen was fouled. McFadyen then scored again, helping Georgians take a lead into halftime at 3-2.

The second half was just as dramatic, with both teams trading goals. Despite several injuries affecting Georgians, McFadyen shone brightly, netting four goals in total. The match concluded with Georgians scoring their sixth goal late in stoppage time, securing an impressive victory. This win keeps them firmly in the play-off spots with 11 games left in the season. Final score: 6-4, with McFadyen earning the Man of the Match award. Attendance was 102.
