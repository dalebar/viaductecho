The past year has seen shocking crime stories unfold across Greater Manchester, with the Manchester Evening News covering harrowing cases from murder to political misconduct. Notable incidents include the gruesome murder of Stuart Everett by Marcin Majerkiewicz, who was sentenced to life with a minimum of 34 years. Former MP Mike Amesbury faced public shame after assaulting a man, receiving a suspended sentence. A significant trial in Rochdale saw seven men convicted for the grooming and rape of two girls, highlighting their exploitation and the heartbreaking nature of the crimes. The murders of Thomas Campbell and Niall Johnson by those close to them shed light on horrific family betrayals, with both perpetrators receiving long sentences. In a tragic incident, young Louisa Palmisano was killed by a drug-fuelled driver. The year also marked the downfall of gangster Jamie Rothwell, sentenced for extensive drug and gun crimes after being caught through law enforcement advancements. As 2025 draws to a close, these cases reflect ongoing struggles with crime and justice in the region, showcasing the bravery of victims and the resilience of the justice system.
