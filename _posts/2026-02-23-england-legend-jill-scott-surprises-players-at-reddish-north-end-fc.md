Jill Scott, the England football legend, recently surprised players at Reddish North End FC as part of a campaign focused on improving nutritional advice for young athletes. Recognising the vital role nutrition played in her career, Scott shared insights influenced by England coach Sarina Wiegman, who pioneered a 'nutritional revolution' in women's football. During her visit, she helped prepare a healthy meal with M&S Food and club volunteers, highlighting how performance is rooted off the pitch. Emphasising the importance of grassroots football, Scott mentioned how nurturing young talents can lead them to become better players. Club chairman John Hargreaves expressed gratitude for community support, while cook Bethany Dugdale noted the club's long-standing role in helping local kids stay active and healthy. With over 1,200 members, Reddish North End FC continues to be a cornerstone of the community.
