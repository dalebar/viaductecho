Ofsted's recent inspections of children’s services in Greater Manchester reveal various quality ratings across schools and nurseries. Reports show strengths and weaknesses, with some notable mentions:

1. **Chaddy Kids Club** was cited for gaps in safeguarding training and hygiene practices, despite praise for resources and staff support.
   
2. **Chances Mentoring**, an independent school, was commended for a well-adapted curriculum and effective safeguarding, though it has few pupils.

3. **Willow House School** improved its curriculum significantly and meets most standards, yet retains a ‘requires improvement’ rating.

4. **Acorns School** showed marked progress in curriculum development, also rated as ‘requires improvement’.

5. **Kiddi Day Kare Audenshaw** faced criticism for leadership weaknesses impacting safeguarding but was noted for happy children and supportive staff.

6. **Lord’s School** maintained issues with subject planning, receiving an ‘inadequate’ rating.

7. **Cambian Chesham House School** showed improvement in behaviour management, now rated ‘requires improvement’.

8. **Partington Nursery** received a ‘good’ rating, praised for a supportive environment and inclusivity efforts.
