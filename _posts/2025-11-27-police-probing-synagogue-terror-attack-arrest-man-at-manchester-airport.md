A 31-year-old man has been arrested at Manchester Airport in connection with the recent terror attack at Heaton Park Hebrew Congregation Synagogue. Greater Manchester Police confirmed that he was detained on suspicion of terrorism-related offences after arriving on an inbound flight. This brings the total arrests linked to the attack to seven. The incident occurred on October 2, resulting in the deaths of Adrian Daulby and Melvin Cravitz, with three others sustaining serious injuries. Assistant Chief Constable Rob Potts has urged anyone with information or who witnessed the attack to come forward for assistance. The investigation is ongoing, and authorities are committed to providing welfare support to witnesses.
