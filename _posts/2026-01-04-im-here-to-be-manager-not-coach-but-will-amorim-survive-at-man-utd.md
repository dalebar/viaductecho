Ruben Amorim's had a pop at Manchester United's management, urging them to let him get on with his job without butting in. He's been under fire from former players like Neville, who are voicing their concerns about the club. When asked about his standing with the hierarchy, Amorim hinted that selective information has been floating around. Following a game at Elland Road, he chose to skedaddle from the media room instead of clarifying his remarks. On top of that, he's dealing with the struggles of £73.7m striker, Benjamin Sesko, which isn’t doing him any favours.
