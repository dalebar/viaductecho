Bolton council has responded to a tragic crash on Wigan Road that claimed four lives, including three teenagers and a taxi driver. They expressed their commitment to support those affected and investigate contributing factors. Residents have started a petition for speed cameras and safety measures on the road, where high-speed driving has been a concern. A recent police investigation includes a video showing a car allegedly reaching 122mph before the crash. The community is rallying for action, pointing out the history of accidents and calling for urgent safety measures, as multiple incidents in the past have been reported. The council pledged to work with emergency services and undertake a detailed review, aiming to implement any recommendations that arise from the inquiry. The local council highlighted the community's heartbreaking loss, emphasising the need for effective action following this devastating event.
