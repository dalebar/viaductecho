A shooting in Urmston, Greater Manchester, on Friday night has left one person seriously injured, prompting an ongoing investigation. Armed police and emergency services responded to reports of a firearms discharge on Higher Road at around 8:42pm. A cordon was established near the railway station as paramedics treated the victim at the scene. Witnesses described a ripped puffa jacket on the pavement, suggesting that the injured individual received significant treatment. Local businesses expressed shock, noting that such incidents are rare in the area. Extra police patrols have been deployed, and authorities are urging anyone with information or relevant footage to contact Greater Manchester Police. The scene has since cleared, but enquiries are still underway. Chief Inspector Mark Allinson confirmed the seriousness of the situation and highlighted that the investigation is ongoing.
