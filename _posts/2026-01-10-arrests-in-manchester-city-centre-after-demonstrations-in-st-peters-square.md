Police in Manchester city centre have made several arrests following protests in St Peter's Square, where around 400 people gathered for three demonstrations. Greater Manchester Police (GMP) reported that while the protests were largely peaceful, one man was arrested for racially aggravated assault and another for affray. Both suspects remain in custody as of Saturday evening. The police thanked the public for their patience, noting that they worked throughout the day to support the protests while minimizing disruption. Overall, GMP's city centre division confirmed the conclusion of the demonstrations and assured continued police presence to maintain safety in the area.
