Bobby Gillespie paid a heartfelt tribute to his late bandmate Gary 'Mani' Mounfield during his funeral at Manchester Cathedral. Following a moving speech from Ian Brown of The Stone Roses, Bobby reminisced about Mani’s vibrant spirit, describing him as a "funky little mother" with a rock-and-roll heart. He praised Mani's talent and his friendly nature, which he extended to everyone regardless of status. Bobby shared fond memories of their time in Primal Scream, emphasising Mani’s contagious enthusiasm and love for life. He also acknowledged Mani’s wife Imelda, who passed away from cancer, and highlighted Mani's deep pride in his Irish roots and Manchester heritage, affectionately calling it "Manichester." The service was well-attended by friends and music industry icons, underscoring Mani’s impact. Ian Brown reflected on Mani’s joyful personality and the profound loss felt by those who knew him, expressing that there would never be another like him in their lives. The ceremony carried a strong sense of celebration for Mani's life, marked by love, laughter, and memories in true Mancunian spirit.
