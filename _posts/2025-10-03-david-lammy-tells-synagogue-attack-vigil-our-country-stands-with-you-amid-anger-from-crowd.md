At a vigil for victims of the Manchester synagogue attack, Deputy Prime Minister David Lammy expressed solidarity, affirming that "our country stands with you" and pledging to combat terrorism. Despite facing protests accusing him of culpability, he encouraged unity among the community. Lammy spoke about the broader impact of the attack, highlighting fears within the Jewish community and the resilience they exhibit. During the event, Rabbi Daniel Walker acknowledged the recovery of a community hero and two individuals who bravely confronted the attacker. He led prayers for the injured and remembered the victims, emphasizing their tragic loss on Yom Kippur. Overall, the event was a heartfelt call for togetherness and resistance against hate.
