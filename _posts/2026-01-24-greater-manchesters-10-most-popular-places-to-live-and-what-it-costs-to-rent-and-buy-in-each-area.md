The top 10 places to live in Greater Manchester have been revealed, highlighting key neighbourhoods along with property costs. Ancoats tops the list, attracting 8% of movers with a trendy vibe and an average house price of £301,143, plus monthly rent of £1,334. Didsbury follows closely, popular with families and professionals, boasting the highest property prices at £428,837 and rent around £1,780. Worsley ranks third, known for its charm, with an average house price of £300,792 and monthly rent of £1,260. 

Chorlton shines with a massive 44.6% rise in movers, while other notable areas include Hulme (£192,141 to buy, £1,363 rent), Manchester city centre (£256,471 to buy, £1,354 rent), and Old Trafford (£360,535, £1,105 rent). Urmston offers suburban peace with a house price of £344,831 and rent of £1,135. Denton is the affordable choice at £220,178 to buy and £1,001 rent, and Eccles rounds out the list with prices at £268,468 to buy and £1,117
