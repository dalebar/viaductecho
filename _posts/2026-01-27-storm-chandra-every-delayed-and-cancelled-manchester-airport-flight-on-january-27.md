Storm Chandra is hitting the UK hard today with heavy rain and strong winds. The Met Office has issued yellow and amber warnings for various regions, particularly for rain and snow in the northern areas, including parts of the Pennines and Scotland. Greater Manchester is currently just under a rain warning, experiencing heavy showers this morning that should ease a bit by 11am but will persist into the afternoon. Wind gusts are expected to reach up to 21mph. This weather may cause some travel disruptions, especially for flights. Jet2 has alerted passengers that there could be delays, though they’re trying their best to stick to schedules. On Tuesday, January 27, several flights from Manchester Airport are cancelled, including a Finnair flight to Helsinki and an Aer Lingus flight to Belfast, with others experiencing significant delays. If you’re flying, best to check your flight status!
