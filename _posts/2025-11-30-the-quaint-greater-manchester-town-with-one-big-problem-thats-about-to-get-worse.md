Littleborough, a charming town on the edge of Greater Manchester, is facing backlash over plans for 309 new homes approved on former green belt land near a notorious traffic hotspot. Residents are already frustrated with the existing traffic woes during rush hour, and worries have grown about how these new developments will exacerbate congestion. Local councillors and over 1,000 residents have voiced opposition, citing traffic issues, overdevelopment, and strain on local services like schools and healthcare as major concerns. Some locals have suggested that new road infrastructure is necessary before more homes can be accommodated. While Bloor Homes, the developer, champions the project for providing affordable housing and community improvements, many locals remain skeptical about the benefits, fearing that the lovely town might lose its charm under the strain of increased traffic and overcrowding.
