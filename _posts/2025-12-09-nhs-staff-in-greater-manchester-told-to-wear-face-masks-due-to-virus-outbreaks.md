NHS staff at some Greater Manchester hospitals are being asked to wear face masks to curb the virus spread, especially as flu cases are rising in the region. While Covid-19 rates are low, hospitals are experiencing high demand, with some, like Royal Oldham, reporting patients waiting over 50 hours for a bed. The increase in respiratory syncytial virus (RSV) adds to the pressure, prompting local health bosses to review infection control measures ahead of the busy Christmas period. Manchester University NHS Foundation Trust has already implemented mask-wearing for A&E staff when treating patients with respiratory issues. They emphasised that patient and staff safety is paramount and that precautions will align with national guidelines.
