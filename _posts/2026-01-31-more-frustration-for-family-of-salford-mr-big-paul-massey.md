The inquest into the death of Salford 'Mr Big' Paul Massey, who was shot in 2015, has faced yet another delay, frustrating his family. They welcomed a review of sensitive police intelligence, hoping it might finally address concerns surrounding his murder. Paul Massey was killed amid a violent gang war involving the A-Team and the Anti A-Team, and his family has fought for a comprehensive hearing for over a decade. Senior coroner Timothy Brennand highlighted the importance of reviewing police intelligence regarding a 'Threat to Life' warning prior to Massey's death. The inquest was initially set to begin on March 23, but complications in appointing a specially-vetted barrister led to the postponement. Massey's daughter Kelly expressed her frustration but remained determined to seek justice for her father, stating that continued efforts are necessary.
